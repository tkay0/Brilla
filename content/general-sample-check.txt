ROUND 2
Q. A chromatographic experiment is to be performed to separate a solid mixture of two differently coloured compounds. The solids are only slightly soluble in petroleum ether but very soluble in chloroform. A column has been prepared for you. This is a glass tube containing the adsorbent in petroleum ether. Describe exactly how you would proceed to perform the experiment and separate the mixture. (C)

A.
1. Weigh the desired amount of the solid mixture.
2. Dissolve it in the minimum volume of chloroform.
3. Drain the column until the solvent level drops to the level of the adsorbent.
4. Using a Pasteur pipette, transfer the concentrated chloroform solution to the top of the column.
5. Let the solution drain to the level of the adsorbent.
6. Add more petroleum ether and collect the eluent at the rate of about a drop per second.
7. When the two colours are separated, increase the polarity of the solvent/eluent.
8. Collect 5 or 10 cm3 fractions.
9. Combine fractions of the same colour.
10. Distil off most of the solvents and leave concentrated solutions in the fridge to crystallize or distil off all the solvents, combine residues of the same colour and recrystallize residues in suitable solvents.
